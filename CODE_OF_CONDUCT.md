# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the WAF Troubleshooting Playbook community a respectful and constructive experience for everyone, regardless of age, background, experience level, nationality, or any other personal characteristic.

We pledge to act in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, and technically focused community.

---

## Our Standards

**Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Sharing knowledge generously and accurately
- Giving and receiving technical feedback constructively
- Acknowledging when you are uncertain or when information may be incomplete
- Crediting others' contributions and prior work
- Being patient with contributors at different experience levels

**Examples of unacceptable behavior:**

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks in any form
- Dismissing contributions based on the contributor rather than the content
- Vendor advocacy or promotional behavior disguised as technical content
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Any other conduct that would be inappropriate in a professional technical setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other venue associated with this repository.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a GitHub issue or contacting the repository maintainer directly via GitHub. All reports will be reviewed and handled with discretion.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and to address behavior that they determine is harmful to the community.
